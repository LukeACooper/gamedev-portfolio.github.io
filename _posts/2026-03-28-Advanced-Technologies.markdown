      content: "For my first project, I developed a mobile WebAR application where players use their device's camera to scan for a suitable surface, and then freely place and anchor a 3D forest diorama directly onto the physical floor, allowing the player to freely look around the model without it moving with the camera. The project also featured an interactive UI, by using ScriptableObjects to generate informational cards. This project aimed to explore the effectiveness of AR as a learning tool, and I decided wildlife conservation fit my concept best."
    - title: "Eye Tracking as a Game Mechanic"
      content: "This project involved using webcam-based eye tracking software in tandem with Unity, my interperetation was to create a series of micro-games, in a fast paced, 2D game similar in concept to the WarioWare games. I designed and modelled a marbe maze, using head tilt data to rotate the maze to complete the puzzle. Also integrated eye tracking data to control the menu UI, and a hoop and wire game, where the player's gaze must follow a trail of points to move the hoop to the other side."
